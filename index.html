what is is his breed?

Napoli and Villarreal are all set to lock horns in the Round of 32 in the Europa League and I'm far from happy about it. Don't get me wrong, I'm eagerly anticipating the first leg at the Stadio San Paolo tonight, but to all intents and purposes this should be a Champions League encounter. The star power and talent that will be on display deserves a stage that is befitting of the occasion like Leonard Cohen at the Isle of Wight in 1970 or Nirvana at the Reading Festival in '92. Both teams sit in the top four of their respective leagues and have won over pundits and fans alike with their carefree brand of free-flowing football. Just as some people like to complain that they were born in the wrong decade, fate has handed the Yellow Submarine a raw deal. Their task consists of trying to dethrone Barcelona, who are the most aesthetically pleasing outfit on the planet and possess more attacking talent than Alexander the Great's famed Companion Cavalry. Still, this has not stopped Juan Carlos Garrido's side from playing champagne football in La Liga and they are primed to seal a top four finish at the season's end despite a recent two-game blip that has seen them relinquish third spot to Valencia. Villarreal are no strangers to the Champions League and were a missed Juan Roman Riquelme penalty away from forcing extra time in the semi-finals against Arsenal in the 2005-06 edition of the tournament.

Villarreal are no strangers to the Champions League and were a missed Juan Roman Riquelme penalty away from forcing extra time in the semi-finals against Arsenal in the 2005-06 edition of the tournament. While Napoli were in the Serie C1/B when Jens Lehmann pushed away Riquelme's spot kick on that fateful April evening, they have made a meteoric rise to the upper echelons of Italian football and are currently second in the top-flight table.

Random fact about him

Villarreal are certainly not devoid of any firepower up front, with Giuseppe Rossi scoring goals for fun, Santi Cazorla playing with a chip on his shoulder on his comeback tour after a back injury last season and Borja Valero enjoying a magnificent campaign at El Madrigal. As a result, I will watch the match this evening with one eye toward the future. It will be like checking out a band in a sweaty, small nightclub where everyone in attendance knows that they are destined for bigger and better things.
